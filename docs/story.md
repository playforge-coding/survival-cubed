# The Story

Long ago, before the night ever turned against the living, the **good king** ruled
the overworld in peace. His was a quiet, sunlit reign — knights kept his roads,
his people farmed the plains, and the only dark thing in the world stayed where it
belonged: deep beneath the surface, in the **underworld**, behind the demons it
held.

## The betrayal

The peace did not break by force. It broke by a whisper.

One of the king's most **trusted advisors** was approached — and tricked — by the
**demon king**, the ruler of the underworld. The demon king offered a bargain no
honest man should have taken: open the way, and let **every demon in the underworld
pour up into the overworld**. In exchange, the advisor would be crowned **demon
king of the overworld** — a throne above the surface to mirror the demon king's
throne below. Two kingdoms of the damned, one beneath the world and one upon it,
and the advisor would rule the half that had once been his home.

He agreed. The way was opened. The demons came up, and the good king was
overthrown.

## The kingdom divided

The land split the moment the new king took the throne.

- The **[knights](entities/knight.md)** who had sworn to the old crown would not
  bend the knee to a usurper crowned by demons. Some of them **left to fight for
  whoever still stood** against the new order — masterless blades, sellswords,
  mercenaries. That is what a wandering knight on the plains is: a free knight,
  still loyal to a kingdom that no longer exists.
- Others **stayed with the new king**. They took his colours and his cause and
  became the **[dark knights](entities/dark-knight.md)** — and to them, every free
  knight is a deserter to be cut down.
- **Many were thrown into prison**, cast down into the underworld the demons had
  emptied. The charred dark did not hold them so much as **remake** them: over long
  years it mutated the prisoners into the hulking [orcs](entities/orcs.md), and the
  court wizards among them into [orc mages](entities/orcs.md#orc-mage). Those who
  didn't last long enough to fully change were burned to bone instead — the
  [charred skeletons](entities/skeletons.md#charred-skeleton) that still walk the
  ash valleys.

## The forgetting

The new king did not want his crime remembered.

The orcs lost **most of what they had seen** — the deeds of the demons, the
bargain, the face of the advisor who became king. The mutation took it from them
along with everything else. And the few prisoners who **did** still remember were
**forbidden to speak of it**, on pain of whatever the underworld does to those who
disobey. So the truth sank down with them into the dark, half-erased and half-
silenced, and the surface forgot there had ever been a good king at all.

## Decades later — you

You arrive when it has been **decades** since the betrayal.

The overworld has had a long time to rot. Most of the demons that once poured up
out of the underworld are no longer recognisable as demons: they have **possessed
the creatures already living on the surface**, wearing the shapes of things that
belong here. The monsters that hunt you in the night are not all invaders from
below — many are the overworld's own animals, ridden by the demons that came up
generations ago and never left.

That is the world you wake into. The good king is long gone, the new king's reign
is the only one anyone remembers, and the truth of how it happened is buried in the
underworld with the orcs who were forbidden to tell it.
