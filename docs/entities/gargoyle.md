# Gargoyle

A squat **stone** fiend of the underworld's charred expanse. A gargoyle **never
walks** — it sits gathered on the rock, then **springs** in short hops, closing on you
one leap at a time. When you stray near it gathers itself and **jump-slams**, trying to
crash down right on top of you. Worst of all, it is carved from stone: **no weapon but a
pickaxe** can so much as chip it.

## Gargoyle

| Stat | Value |
|---|---|
| **Health** | 32 HP |
| **Size** | 10 × 15 px |
| **Movement** | **Hops only** — springs in ~60 px/s arcs, gathering between leaps |
| **Damage** | 12 HP jump slam (lands on you) |
| **Found in** | Underworld **charred** expanse — slightly **rarer than [demons](demons.md)** |
| **Activity** | All hours |
| **Weakness** | **Pickaxe only** — every other weapon glances off its stone hide |

A gargoyle has no walk cycle at all. It holds still, gathered low to the ground, then
**hops** toward you in a short arc — sit, spring, sit, spring — chasing one leap at a
time. Left alone it hops idly about its patch of charred rock; the moment you come within
about 240 px it starts springing your way.

### Jump slam

Once you're within roughly **90 px**, the gargoyle commits: it gathers and **leaps high
toward you**, aiming the arc to come down **on top of you**, and the blow lands the
instant it touches the ground for **12 HP**. It aims where you *are* as it launches, so a
gargoyle mid-leap can be sidestepped — keep moving and it crashes down on empty rock,
then needs a beat to recover before it can spring again.

!!! danger "Bring a pickaxe"
    A gargoyle is **stone**. A sword, an axe, a bare fist — all of them simply **bounce
    off**. Only a **[pickaxe](../crafting.md)** bites into it, the same tool you'd swing
    at the rock it's carved from. Caught in the charred dark with nothing but a blade, you
    cannot hurt a gargoyle at all — back away from its slam and come back with a mining
    tool. A tungsten pickaxe cracks one open fastest.

Gargoyles are a touch **rarer than demons** — roughly one charred-expanse chunk in seven
seeds one — and, like the demons, they keep to the main charred rock and shun the ash
valleys. They don't hit as hard as the underworld's brutes, but a stone hopper you can't
scratch without the right tool is a nasty surprise for an unprepared traveller.

## Lore

The gargoyles were **carved to stand guard**.

The [demon king](demon-king.md) had them hewn from the living stone of the underworld to
**protect his throne** — squat, patient sentinels that would sit motionless at his gates
for years and spring to life the instant anything unwelcome drew near. For a long age
they did exactly that, ringing the deep dark around their master.

But stone sentinels left to themselves grow **restless** in their own slow way. A roving
few **hopped off into the charred expanse** on some errand no one set them, drifting
further and further from the throne they were made to keep — and **never returned to
their master again**. They are still out there now, springing through the dark, guarding
nothing, slamming down on anything that wanders too close out of a habit older than they
can remember.

The **[good king](../story.md)** had gargoyles of his own once, set to watch over his
sunlit hall the way the demon king's watched over his. But when the way was opened and the
demons poured up, his stone guardians stood against the tide — and were **shattered to the
last during the war**. Not one of the old king's gargoyles survived the fall of his
kingdom. Every gargoyle left in the world is the demon king's: a stray sentinel that wandered
off and forgot what it was guarding.
