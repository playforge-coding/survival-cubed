# Dark Musketeer

A black-clad marksman that marches under the **[demon king](demon-king.md)'s**
banner — the ranged twin of the [dark knight](dark-knight.md). It fights like one,
hanging back and firing **bullets**, but it makes war on [knights](knight.md) and
[musketeers](musketeer.md) as readily as on you, firing at all three.

| Stat | Value |
|---|---|
| **Health** | 40 HP |
| **Size** | 11 × 14 px |
| **Speed** | 24 px/s |
| **Damage** | 30 HP per bullet (~2.6 s between shots) |
| **Found in** | Summoned by the enraged demon king (the **arena**) |
| **Activity** | Always active in the arena |

## Behaviour

The dark musketeer is a **ranged kiter**. It keeps its distance (closing to about
95 px) and fires **bullets** at its target from as far as ~210 px. Like the friendly
[musketeer](musketeer.md) its musket is **slow but hard-hitting** — each bullet lands
a heavy **30 HP**, but it reloads slowly (~2.6 s between shots). The bullet flies fast
in a straight line until it hits its mark or a wall, so **strafe behind cover** or
rush it down between shots.

It hunts **players, [knights](knight.md), and [musketeers](musketeer.md)** alike —
whichever is nearest. Like the other dark warriors it **burns up at daybreak** in
the overworld, though the ever-dark arena it is summoned into keeps it fighting
around the clock.

## Where it appears

You meet dark musketeers in the **[demon king](demon-king.md)** fight. Past
two-thirds health the king **enrages**, calling down a host of **two
[dark knights](dark-knight.md) and two dark musketeers** to swarm you alongside it.

## Restoring it

A dark musketeer caught by the **[restore spell](../magic.md#the-restore-spell)**
becomes a loyal **[musketeer](musketeer.md)**, recruited to you — turning one of
the king's gunners into your own. It is the only way to win a musketeer without
finding a wild one on the plains.

## Lore

Where the [musketeers](musketeer.md) abandoned the fallen crown, these did not.
When the **original king** was overthrown, the dark musketeers **stuck with the
new, evil king**, pledging their aim to the usurper — which is why they fire on
their old comrades as readily as on you.
