# Knight

A lone man-at-arms who wanders the **plains** — a rare sight, found in only about
**1 in 33 plains chunks**, far scarcer than the horses that share the grass. You
**can't attack it**. Recruit it with a **tungsten ingot** to win a true companion
that fights monsters at your side, even across dimensions.

| Stat | Value |
|---|---|
| **Health** | 40 HP |
| **Size** | 10 × 13 px |
| **Speed** | 40 px/s on foot, 64 px/s mounted |
| **Damage** | 12 HP per blow (~0.7 s between swings) |
| **Found in** | Plains (surface), rare |
| **Recruited with** | A tungsten ingot |
| **Activity** | Day & night |

## Wild knights

Even before you recruit it, a wild knight is no bystander: it **charges any
monster** that strays near and trades blows until one of them falls — and the
monsters hunt it right back, the same way they hunt you. A wild knight caught
alone against a night swarm can be **overwhelmed and slain**, so reach a promising
one before the dark does. **A wild knight killed in battle is gone for good.**

## Recruiting

Hold a [tungsten ingot](../crafting.md#smelting) and left-click the knight to
**recruit it**, spending the ingot. A recruited knight is yours:

- **It fights for you.** It charges the nearest monster on its own, and if you
  strike an enemy your knight makes that foe its priority — trading heavy blows
  until it falls.
- **It follows you everywhere** — even **across dimensions**. Step through a fire
  key and your knight comes too. Stray too far (~360 px) and it teleports to your
  side, like a pet.
- **It rides into battle.** If a **wild** [horse](horse.md) is near, the knight
  mounts up and gallops — and about **3 in 10 knights are already mounted** when
  you find them. The horse shields it, soaking every blow until slain, after which
  the knight fights on foot. (It won't take a horse you've tamed.)
- **Death breaks the bond.** Unlike a pet, a slain *recruited* knight respawns at
  **your** respawn point as a *wild* knight — you must **recruit it again** with
  another tungsten ingot.

!!! tip "Let the knight take the hits"
    Monsters are hostile to knights as well as to you. Let a knight soak the blows
    while you strike from behind it — and if it's mounted, the horse soaks them
    first.

## Lore

These were the sworn knights of the **original king**, the one who was
**overthrown**. When the crown fell they refused to bend the knee to the usurper
and left to wander as **mercenaries** — masterless blades selling their loyalty to
whoever proves worthy of it. That is why a tungsten ingot can buy what no command
could: a knight follows you because you earned it, not because a king ordered it.
