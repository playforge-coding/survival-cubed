# Farmer

A friendly folk who wanders the **plains** and makes a living off the land's
livestock — a rare sight, found in only about **1 in 25 plains chunks**. You
**can't attack it**. Hand it an **iron ingot** and it trades back a windfall of
food, either way more than the ingot is worth.

| Stat | Value |
|---|---|
| **Health** | 20 HP |
| **Size** | 10 × 13 px |
| **Speed** | Ambles at 30 px/s; bolts at 64 px/s from danger |
| **Damage** | 6 HP per strike to livestock (~0.8 s between blows) — never harms players |
| **Found in** | Plains (surface), rare |
| **Trades for** | An iron ingot |
| **Activity** | Day & night |

## What it does

A farmer is a **livestock hunter**, not a fighter. Left alone it works its rounds:

- **It culls the herd.** It charges the nearest [chicken or goat](livestock.md),
  striking it down like a [puppy](pets.md) hunts, then **collects** the raw meat
  the animal drops (eating it on the spot, healing a little).
- **It flees monsters.** A farmer has no stomach for a fight — when any monster
  strays near, it **drops everything and runs**, fast enough to outpace most of
  what hunts it. Monsters hunt a farmer the same way they hunt you or a
  [knight](knight.md), so a farmer caught against a night swarm can be
  **overwhelmed and slain**.
- **It despawns when you leave.** Like the livestock it preys on, a farmer is not
  a permanent fixture — wander far enough and it's culled, with fresh ones seeded
  into new plains as you explore.

## Trading

Hold an [iron ingot](../crafting.md#smelting) and left-click the farmer to
**trade**, spending the ingot. In return it hands over **one** of these at
random — you never know which until you trade:

- **8 [apples](../crafting.md#food)**, or
- **4 [cooked meat](../crafting.md#food)**.

!!! tip "A reliable food supply"
    A single iron ingot for eight apples or four ready-to-eat meals is a generous
    deal. Once you're smelting iron in bulk, a farmer on your home plains is a
    steady larder — no cooking required for the meat.

## Lore

When the old kingdom fell, not everyone took up a sword like the wandering
[knights](knight.md). The farmers simply kept doing what they always had — working
the land, tending the herds — and learned to keep one eye on the treeline for
whatever the dark might send their way.
