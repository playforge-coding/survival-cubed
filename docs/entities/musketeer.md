# Musketeer

A wandering marksman who roams the **plains** — the ranged twin of the
[knight](knight.md), found in about **1 in 33 plains chunks**. You **can't attack
it**. Recruit it with a **tungsten ingot** to win a companion that guns down
monsters from range at your side, even across dimensions.

| Stat | Value |
|---|---|
| **Health** | 34 HP |
| **Size** | 11 × 14 px |
| **Speed** | 30 px/s |
| **Damage** | 30 HP per bullet (~2.6 s between shots) |
| **Found in** | Plains (surface), rare; or by restoring a [dark musketeer](dark-musketeer.md) |
| **Recruited with** | A tungsten ingot |
| **Activity** | Day & night |

## Wild musketeers

Like a wild knight, a wild musketeer is no bystander: it **hunts any monster**
that strays near, kiting it from range and loosing **friendly bullets** until one
of them falls — and the monsters hunt it right back, the same way they hunt you. A
wild musketeer caught alone against a night swarm can be **overwhelmed and slain**,
so reach a promising one before the dark does. **A wild musketeer killed in battle
is gone for good.**

## Behaviour

The musketeer is a **ranged kiter**. It locks onto the nearest monster within
~270 px, closes to firing range (~210 px), and backs away if the monster slips
inside ~90 px. Its musket is a **slow, hard-hitting** weapon — a fast lead ball that
flies straight until it strikes a monster or a wall, dealing a heavy **30 HP**, but
it reloads slowly (~2.6 s between shots).

## Recruiting

Hold a [tungsten ingot](../crafting.md#smelting) and left-click the musketeer to
**recruit it**, spending the ingot. A recruited musketeer is yours:

- **It fights for you.** It hunts the nearest monster on its own, gunning it down
  from a safe distance.
- **It follows you everywhere** — even **across dimensions**. Stray too far and it
  teleports to your side, like a knight.
- **Death breaks the bond.** Like a knight, a slain *recruited* musketeer respawns
  at **your** respawn point as a *wild* musketeer — you must **recruit it again**
  with another tungsten ingot.

!!! tip "A gunline behind your knights"
    Pair a musketeer with a [knight](knight.md): let the knight close and soak the
    blows in melee while the musketeer fires over its shoulder from the back rank.

## Lore

The musketeers carried the **original king's** firearms — a newer art than the
sworn blade, but the same broken oath. When the crown fell they too left to wander
as mercenaries, selling their aim to whoever proves worthy of it. A tungsten ingot
buys their loyalty, just as it buys a knight's.
