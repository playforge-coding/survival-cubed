# Mage

A robed spellcaster that fights with magic instead of steel. A mage is never born
into the world and never spawns on its own — the **only** way one comes to be is the
**[restore spell](../magic.md)**, cast on an **[orc mage](orcs.md)** to redeem the
brute caster into this gentler one.

| Stat | Value |
|---|---|
| **Health** | 30 HP |
| **Size** | 9 × 12 px |
| **Speed** | 38 px/s |
| **Found in** | Created only by the [restore spell](../magic.md) |
| **Activity** | Day & night |

## A caster at your side

A mage you create with the restore spell is **recruited** to you, just like a
[knight](knight.md): it follows you everywhere it can — even across dimensions — and
**nothing can harm it** (no monster targets it, and a swing simply bounces off).

What sets a mage apart is that it **casts the world's spells on its own**, on a
steady cadence, choosing what the moment calls for:

- **[Restore](../magic.md)** — its first choice against a foe it can turn (an orc,
  dark knight, orc mage, or enchanted demon).
- **[Sunburst](../magic.md)** — when daylight-burning undead crowd nearby.
- **[Summoner](../magic.md)** — a friendly skull flung at any other monster.

!!! tip "A mage builds your retinue"
    When a **recruited** mage casts restore, the new knight or mage is recruited **on
    your behalf** — so a single mage left to its work can hand you a growing band of
    knights and fellow mages. A **wild** mage's restorations stay wild.

## Wild mages

A mage only stays wild if a *wild* mage created it (a recruited mage always recruits
to its owner). A wild mage roams its patch and still casts helpfully against
monsters, but its restorations join no one.
