# Twinscale

The **post-game superboss** of the [arena](../world.md#the-arena) — a colossal
**twin-headed blue dragon**, and the hardest fight in the world. It is raised only
**after the [Demon King](demon-king.md) has been slain**, appearing **five days**
later when a challenger next steps into the arena room.

| Stat | Value |
|---|---|
| **Health** | 1600 HP |
| **Size** | 118 × 72 px — by far the largest creature in the world |
| **Speed** | 60 px/s (flies) |
| **Found in** | The **arena**, five days after the demon king falls — one per world |
| **Activity** | Always active; never despawns |
| **Drops** | 10 dragon scales, 6 gold ingots, 6 tungsten ingots where it falls |

## You need to fly

Twinscale **holds station high** beneath the arena room's tall ceiling and never
descends — that is what the room's high roof is for. Ground attacks can't reach it:
a musket bullet fizzles out long before it climbs that high, and melee is hopeless.

**The only way to fight Twinscale is to fly up to it** on a **[white dragon
steed](../magic.md#the-dragonian-steed-spell)**. Cast the **dragonian steed spell**
(found in the demon king's chest), **ride** your steed up into the air, and breathe
fire on Twinscale at close range while you dodge its volleys. Note that the arena
**bars telepathic piloting** — you must actually *ride* the steed up, putting
yourself in the air with it, not steer it from safety on the floor.

!!! danger "Bring the steed"
    Without the dragonian steed spell, Twinscale is unbeatable — it simply rains fire
    from a height you cannot reach. Make sure you have looted (and ideally
    [cloned](../crafting.md)) the spell before the five days are up.

## Behaviour

Twinscale **flies straight at you**, holding its high altitude but closing in to
hover right overhead — you cannot park in one spot and let every volley miss; you
have to keep moving and out-fly it. On a cadence it looses one of three attacks,
each telegraphed by a wind-up before it releases:

- **Fireball storm** — a wide fan of **ten** [fireballs](demons.md#demon) raining down.
- **Magic barrage** — a spread of **six** [magic fireballs](demons.md#enchanted-demon),
  which fly farther and hit harder.
- **Summoner volley** — **two** [summoner bolts](necromancer.md#necromancer), each
  bursting into a bouncing [skull](necromancer.md#skull).

If you tuck in **directly underneath** Twinscale — the blind spot a wide fan would
arc straight over — it abandons its cadence entirely and pours an **unbroken stream
of magic fireballs straight down** onto you, with no wind-up and no pause between
bolts, for as long as you stay below it. Hiding right under it is no longer safe; the
only way to break the stream is to **slide out from under it**.

Its range is enormous, so it can pelt the floor even before you take to the air —
pressure that pushes you up onto your steed to engage.

### Half health: the flight of dragons

When you wound Twinscale to **half health**, it calls down a **flight of three
[dragons](dragon.md)** to harry you through the air alongside it. Now you are dodging
the superboss *and* three minibosses at once — the high point of the fight. Thin the
dragons out (your steed's fire works on them too) or focus the boss and ride through
the storm.

## Lore

Twinscale was the **demon king's greatest creation**. Long before the war, the king
was a breeder of dragons — and an obsessive one. He bred the biggest beasts he could
find to the biggest, generation after generation, each clutch larger and fiercer than
the last, chasing a dragon greater than any that had ever lived. At the end of that
long line came **Twinscale**: a dragon so vast, and bred so far beyond nature, that it
hatched with **two heads** and scales of deep, cold **blue**. It was the king's pride
and his masterwork — and it slept on, deep beneath the arena, even after he fell.
Five days after his death it wakes to avenge its maker.
